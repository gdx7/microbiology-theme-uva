# Group Leader's Guide to Editing Your Research Group Page

**A Simple, Step-by-Step Guide (No Coding Required!)**

---

## Getting Started

You can edit your research group page, add team members with photos, and post news updates **without writing any code**. Everything happens through a user-friendly web interface that looks like a form.

### What You Need:
1. A GitHub account (free, takes 2 minutes to create)
2. Permission to edit the website (request from Gaurav Dugar)
3. Your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)

---

## Part 1: Setting Up Your GitHub Account (One-Time Only)

### Step 1: Create a GitHub Account

1. Go to https://github.com/signup
2. Enter your email address (use your @uva.nl email)
3. Create a password
4. Choose a username (e.g., "jzhang-uva" or your name)
5. Verify your email address

**That's it!** Send your GitHub username to Gaurav Dugar to get access.

### Step 2: Wait for Access

Gaurav will add you as a collaborator. You'll receive an email invitation - click "Accept invitation."

---

## Part 2: Logging Into the Content Editor

### Step 1: Go to the Admin Page

1. Open your web browser
2. Go to: **https://microbiology-uva.vercel.app/admin**
3. You'll see a login screen

### Step 2: Log In with GitHub

1. Click the **"Login with GitHub"** button
2. If asked, enter your GitHub username and password
3. Click **"Authorize"** when asked
4. You'll see the content editor dashboard

**Bookmark this page** for easy access next time!

---

## Part 3: Editing Your Research Group Page

### Step 1: Find Your Group

1. After logging in, look at the left sidebar
2. Click on **"Research Groups"**
3. Find your group in the list and click on it

### Step 2: Edit the Content

You'll see a form with several fields. Here's what each one does:

#### Basic Information:
- **Group Name**: Your lab's name (e.g., "Gumi Lab")
- **Group Code**: Short abbreviation (e.g., "GL")
- **Research Area**: Choose from dropdown (e.g., "MBMFS")
- **Principal Investigator**: Your name
- **PI Role**: Your title (e.g., "Principal Investigator")
- **Contact Email**: Your email address
- **External URL**: Your personal/lab website (if you have one)

#### Descriptions:
- **Short Description**: 1-2 sentences for the card view
  - Example: "We study microbial communities using multi-omics and computational modeling."

- **Full Description**: Detailed description of your research
  - You can use the formatting buttons to make text **bold**, *italic*, add headers, bullet points, etc.
  - Write it like you would in Word or email

#### Research Focus Areas:
- Click **"Add Research Focus Areas"**
- Type one research topic per line
- Examples:
  - Microbiome analysis
  - Multi-omics integration
  - Computational modeling

#### Keywords:
- Click **"Add Keywords"**
- Type keywords related to your research
- Examples: microbiome, multi-omics, modelling

### Step 3: Save Your Changes

**Important:** There are two buttons at the top:

1. **"Save"** - Saves your work as a draft (not published yet)
   - Use this while you're still working
   - You can come back later to finish

2. **"Publish"** - Makes your changes live on the website
   - The website will automatically update in 2-3 minutes
   - Everyone will see your changes

**Always click "Publish" when you're done!**

## Part 4: Adding Team Members (With Photos!)

### Step 1: Scroll to Team Members Section

In your research group page, scroll down to find **"Team Members"**

### Step 2: Add a New Team Member

1. Click **"Add Team Members"** button
2. Fill in the form that appears:

   - **Name**: Full name (e.g., "John Smith")
   - **Role**: Their position (e.g., "PhD Student", "Postdoc", "Research Assistant")
   - **Email**: Their email address (optional)
   - **Photo**: Click "Choose an image" to upload their photo
     - Supported formats: JPG, PNG
     - Recommended size: 400x400 pixels (square)
     - The system will automatically resize it
   - **Bio**: Short description of their research (optional)

3. Click **"Add"** to add them to the list

### Step 3: Upload Photos

When you click "Choose an image":
1. A file browser window will open
2. Navigate to your photo file
3. Select the photo
4. Click "Open"
5. The photo will upload automatically

**Tips for Photos:**
- Use professional headshots
- Square photos work best
- Keep file size under 5MB
- JPG or PNG format

### Step 4: Removing Team Members

1. Find the team member in the list
2. Click the **"X"** or **"Remove"** button next to their name
3. Click **"Publish"** to save the change

---

## Part 5: Adding Publications

### Step 1: Scroll to Publications Section

In your research group page, scroll down to **"Publications (Recent)"**

### Step 2: Add a Publication

1. Click **"Add Publications"** button
2. Fill in the details:
   - **Title**: Full paper title
   - **Authors**: All authors (e.g., "Smith J, Jones A, Zhang L")
   - **Journal**: Journal name (e.g., "Nature Microbiology")
   - **Year**: Publication year (e.g., 2024)
   - **Link**: DOI or URL to the paper (optional)

3. Click **"Add"**

### Step 3: Reordering Publications

- Click and drag publications to reorder them
- Most recent should be at the top

---

## Part 6: Adding News Items

### Step 1: Go to News Section

1. From the dashboard, click **"News"** in the left sidebar
2. Click **"New News"** button

### Step 2: Fill in the News Form

- **Title**: Headline for your news (e.g., "New Publication in Nature")
- **Publish Date**: Choose date and time
- **Tag**: Category (e.g., "Publication", "Award", "Event", "Announcement")
- **Description**: Short summary (2-3 sentences)
  - This appears on the homepage news rotator
- **Full Content**: Longer article (optional)
  - Use formatting buttons for bold, italic, headers, etc.
- **External Link**: Link to full article, press release, etc. (optional)
- **Featured Image**: Upload an image for the news (optional)
- **Show in Rotator**: Toggle ON to feature on homepage

### Step 3: Publish the News

Click **"Publish"** at the top - your news will appear on the website!

## Part 7: Common Questions

### Q: How long does it take for changes to appear?

**A:** After clicking "Publish", the website automatically rebuilds. This takes **2-3 minutes**. Refresh your browser to see the changes.

### Q: Can I preview my changes before publishing?

**A:** Yes! Click "Save" instead of "Publish". This saves your work without making it public. You can preview by clicking "View Preview" (if available) or ask Gaurav for help.

### Q: What if I make a mistake?

**A:** Don't worry! Every change is saved with version control. Contact Gaurav Dugar, and he can restore a previous version.

### Q: Can I edit someone else's group page?

**A:** No, you should only edit your own research group. If you need to edit another page, contact Gaurav Dugar.

### Q: What if I upload the wrong photo?

**A:** Just upload a new photo - it will replace the old one. Or remove the team member and add them again with the correct photo.

### Q: The "Publish" button isn't working

**A:** Check that:
1. All required fields (marked with *) are filled in
2. You're connected to the internet
3. Try refreshing the page and logging in again

### Q: I forgot my GitHub password

**A:** Go to https://github.com/password_reset and follow the instructions.

## Part 8: Best Practices

### For Team Member Photos:
✅ **DO:**
- Use professional headshots
- Use square (1:1 ratio) photos
- Use good lighting
- Keep file size reasonable (under 5MB)
- Use JPG or PNG format

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use group photos
- Use very large files (over 10MB)
- Use photos with watermarks

### For Descriptions:
✅ **DO:**
- Write in clear, accessible language
- Use bullet points for lists
- Keep sentences concise
- Explain acronyms on first use

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use excessive jargon without explanation
- Write very long paragraphs
- Use ALL CAPS
- Include personal contact details in descriptions

### For News Items:
✅ **DO:**
- Add news regularly (publications, awards, events)
- Use clear, engaging titles
- Include links to full articles
- Add relevant images

❌ **DON'T:**
- Post outdated news (over 1 year old)
- Use clickbait titles
- Post sensitive or confidential information
